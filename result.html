More about leaf curl

What is it?

Chilli leaf curl virus is a DNA virus from the genus Begomovirus and family Geminiviridae.

What deficiency causes leaf curl in chilli leaf?

When plants lack calcium, leaves cannot develop properly and will begin to appear curled or bubbly.

Symptoms

  • Upward curling, puckering, and bunching of leaves
  • The leaves are also reduced in size
  • Severely affected plants produce fewer, smaller, and deformed fruits
  • Significant yield losses

How to Control

  1. Apply Carbofuran 3G @ 4-5 Kg/acre in the main field to control sucking complex and insect vectors selectively
  2. Spray the crop with systemic insecticides, Dimethoate 2ml of Acephate 1g per litre of water.

{% elif predictions == "Leaf Spot" %}

More about Leaf Spot

What is it?

This is a common disease of chilli occurring on the foliage at any stage of the growth.

What deficiency causes leaf curl in chilli leaf?

The fungus attacks the foliage causing characteristic leaf spots and blight. Early blight is first observed on the plants as small, black lesions mostly on the older foliage.

Symptoms

  • Leaves, fruits and stems are affected.
  • Lesions on leaf begin as circular, water-soaked spots.
  • Spots become necrotic with brown centre with chlorotic borders
  • Tissue surrounding the spots may turn yellow.
  • Lesions attain considerable size.

How to Control

  1. Liquid copper fungicides such as Monterey Liqui-Cop or Bonide Liquid Copper Fungicide(fungicides like maneb which contains mancozeb (2.5 g/l), hexaconazole (1 ml/l), tebuconazole and difenoconazole).
  2. Apply every 7-10 days as needed to reduce fungal growth and spread.

More about Powdery Mildew

What is it?

The powdery mildew caused in chilli by Leveillula taurica (Lev.) causing heavy yield loss ranging from 14 to 20 per cent, due to severe defoliation and reduction in photosynthesis, size and number of fruits per plant.

What deficiency causes leaf curl in chilli leaf?

The powdery mildew caused in chilli by Leveillula Taurica, Powdery mildew, mainly caused by the fungus Podosphaera xanthii

Symptoms

  • White powdery coating appears mostly on the lower surface
  • Sometimes the powdery coating can also be seen on the upper surface.
  • Correspondingly on the upper surface yellow patches are seen
  • Drying and shedding of affected leaves.

How to Control

  1. Spray wettable sulphur @ 3 g/l or carbendazim @ 1 g/l three sprays at 15 days interval from the first appearance of symptoms
  2. Spray Wettable sulphur 0.25% or Dinocap (Karathane) 0.05%
